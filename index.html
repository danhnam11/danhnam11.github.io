Danh Nam Nguyen

Postdoctoral Researcher at UNIST

Developing high-performance reactive-flow solvers in OpenFOAM for supercritical/transcritical combustion and stiff chemistry simulations.

I received my Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from Ulsan National Institute of Science and Technology (UNIST) in 2024. My doctoral research involved numerical studies of supercritical CO2 combustion and catalytic ammonia decomposition, with emphasis on multidimensional reactive flow modeling and OpenFOAM code development.

My current research interests include real-fluid combustion, adaptive chemistry solvers, and large-scale reactive-flow simulations, with additional interests in GPU-accelerated CFD and physics-informed neural networks (PINNs).
