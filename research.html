psiEff-Based Acceleration in Real-Fluid Transcritical Flow Simulations

A psiEff-based formulation is introduced to reduce the computational cost of the pressure equation in real-fluid simulations under transcritical conditions. The method improves convergence efficiency by modifying the effective compressibility treatment, reducing pressure iteration cost while maintaining robustness and accuracy in near-critical regimes.

OpenFOAM Development

Gas–Surface Chemistry Coupling Strategy in Catalytic Flows

A segregated coupling strategy is used to handle gas-phase and surface chemistry in catalytic simulations. Surface reactions are treated as a boundary-localized stiff system and integrated independently from the bulk flow. Gas-phase chemistry is then solved within computational cells, followed by solution of the finite-volume transport equations for species evolution.

OpenFOAM Development

coTHERM: Time-Correlated Thermophysical Property Evaluation

The detailed transport model (DTM) in OpenFOAM requires recomputation of thermophysical properties in every cell at each time step, leading to high computational cost. The coTHERM method reduces this cost by exploiting the temporal correlation of thermodynamic states, treating thermophysical properties as locally time-invariant over short intervals while preserving accuracy.

Numerical Approach for Transcritical/Supercritical Flows

Numerical instabilities in high-pressure subsonic flows near critical conditions arise from pseudo-boiling effects and strong density stratification in real-fluid models. A pressure-based formulation with a modified PIMPLE algorithm is used to improve stability, together with a Newton–Bisection coupling scheme to ensure robust convergence in OpenFOAM.
